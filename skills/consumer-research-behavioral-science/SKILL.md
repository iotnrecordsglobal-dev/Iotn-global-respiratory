# Consumer Research & Behavioral Science Division

**IOTN Records' Central Intelligence Engine for Human Decision-Making**

---

## 1. CORE PURPOSE

The Consumer Research & Behavioral Science Division (CRBS) is the label's central nervous system for human decision-making—a permanent, cross-functional intelligence engine that transforms raw behavioral data, qualitative insight, and experimental evidence into **predictive models and actionable strategic directives**.

**Fundamental Question:** *What will the audience do next, and why?*

### The Five Defining Questions

1. **Who is the audience, really?** - Multi-layered psychographic, behavioral, and contextual portrait (identity, motivations, fears, aspirations, social contexts, unconscious biases)
2. **What behavior are they exhibiting?** - Objective, verifiable real-time actions (streams, skips, saves, shares, purchases, attendance, community engagement)
3. **Why are they behaving that way?** - Causal drivers: emotional need, social signal, context, cognitive shortcut, identity performance
4. **What can change the behavior?** - Experimental identification of levers (framing shifts, choice architecture, social proof, pricing)
5. **Which behavior is most valuable?** - Economic prioritization based on fan LTV, superfan conversion, catalog longevity, community health

**Transformation:** From reactive entity to anticipatory organization that forecasts demand, pre-empts churn, designs irresistible offerings, and detects cultural shifts early.

---

## 2. MISSION

**To turn intuition into evidence and evidence into action, by decoding the psychology of music consumption, predicting fan behavior, and designing ethical interventions that maximize mutual value for artists and audiences.**

### Seven Operational Objectives

1. **Identify High-Value Fan Segments** - Build predictive models identifying future superfans after 3 streaming sessions
2. **Understand Song Conversion** - Isolate emotional/contextual/identity factors causing saves vs. passive streams; provide A&R pre-release testing
3. **Forecast Demand** - Develop econometric/behavioral models predicting response to releases, tours, merch, pricing with statistical confidence
4. **Design Pricing & Packaging** - Apply behavioral economics (framing, anchoring, decoy effects) to subscriptions, fan clubs, VIP, merch, tickets
5. **Increase Retention & LTV** - Build churn prediction models; design habit-forming loops (content rituals, community rewards, loyalty streaks)
6. **Reduce Waste** - Eliminate inefficient marketing, touring, merch production, and brand partnerships
7. **Detect Cultural Shifts Early** - Build social listening infrastructure to identify nascent trends 3-6 months before mainstream
